Questions

Before you deploy.

What workspace owners and participants ask most before going live.

Nothing upfront. U4IA charges a 20% platform fee on participant payouts plus a 30% infrastructure fee on verified deal value. You set the milestone amounts. You fund only when outcomes are confirmed in your CRM. There's no seat license, no monthly minimum, and no commitment.
HubSpot is the primary integration today — deal stages, contact creation, meeting verification, and attribution sync happen automatically. Salesforce is on the roadmap. The CRM connection is what powers milestone verification, so payouts only fire when your system confirms the outcome actually happened.
You define the ICP, set milestone payout amounts with floor/ceiling structures, and configure readiness multipliers. Participants are invited — they don't self-serve into your workspace. Every introduction is scored against your ICP before it surfaces as an opportunity. You approve the economics, the audience, and the quality threshold.
U4IA scans metadata — sender domains, subject lines, calendar attendees — to identify warm relationship paths. It does not read email bodies or message content. All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. You can disconnect at any time from your settings, and your scan data is purged within 30 days. Full details are on our Trust Center.
Payouts vary by workspace. Typical structures range from $25 for a qualified meeting booked to $375 for a closed-won deal. Most active participants earn between $5K–$10K/month across multiple workspaces. You see the exact payout breakdown before you make any introduction — nothing is hidden.
Payouts are processed through Tremendous. You'll complete a W9 during onboarding (takes 2 minutes), then choose your payout method — Visa, PayPal, ACH, or gift card. Milestone payouts auto-fire within 48 hours of CRM verification. You don't invoice anyone. The money just shows up.
Referral programs ask partners to remember you exist, find a lead on their own, and submit it through a portal. U4IA does the opposite — it scans participants' existing relationships, surfaces the warm paths that match your ICP, prices the opportunity, and drafts the introduction. Participants don't need to know your product. They just need to know people. The intelligence layer does the rest.
No. Your only job is to make a warm introduction between two people — someone in your network and someone at the workspace owner's company. U4IA drafts the intro message for you. The sales team takes it from there. You're not a rep. You're a connector. If the relationship is warm and the intro is relevant, that's all it takes.
You pay nothing. Payouts are milestone-based and CRM-verified. If a meeting doesn't happen, no payout fires. If a deal doesn't close, you don't pay the deal-close milestone. The entire model is outcome-based — you're deploying CAC, not buying hope. Your cost-per-meeting stays controlled because the floor and ceiling structures you set are enforced automatically.
