02 · The Revenue Motion

Program Subsystems — Solve Real Constraints

These subsystems don't come first — they emerge from revenue motion friction.

⚫
Onboarding
This is their first experience working with us. As an AI company, we can't signal that we're manual, slow, cumbersome, or disjointed. We're building their first impression of what it's like to work with us. Shoot for amazing, not run of the mill.
"What's the first 30 minutes?"
■
Certification
There's valuable work inside the customer journey. A partner sees it as ownable and believes it creates economic upside. Only then will they invest in learning. Certification proves they can hold that responsibility credibly.
"Can they own it?"
◆
Enablement
A partner has earned the right to own part of the motion. They attempt to execute and friction appears — messaging gaps, process confusion, missing assets. That friction kills pipeline. Enablement removes it.
"What's slowing them down?"
▶
Activation
A partner is capable but has competing priorities. Without a trigger, nothing happens. You introduce DFY, DWY, and DIY mechanisms — layered with funded programs, pipeline opportunities, and clear economic upside. This creates urgency.
"What makes them move now?"
Born out of necessity, not premeditation. The motion tells you what to build.

04 · Validation & First Revenue

The Judgment Calls Inside Each Step

Anyone can write "recruit 5 partners." The value is knowing which 5, what to listen for, and when to kill vs scale.
01
Which 5 partners do you pick first?
✓ Right
Partners whose clients already feel the missed-call pain. They sell the problem before we arrive.
✕ Wrong
Partners with the biggest logos or most clients. Size ≠ urgency. Wrong 5 burns the quarter.
02
A partner has friction. What kind?
Training
They believe in the motion but can't execute the messaging. Fix with enablement.
Incentive
They can execute but don't care enough. Redesign the offer or kill.
03
What do you listen for in partner interviews?
Signal
"My clients complain about missed calls." "We lose clients when leads don't convert." This is pull.
Noise
"Sounds interesting, send me info." No urgency = no motion.
04
When do you codify vs keep running loose?
Codify
Same pattern 3+ times across different partners. Not luck — signal.
Wait
Seen it once or twice. Premature structure is the cargo cult trap.
05
How do you know a partner type works?
Works
3+ partners of same type producing pipeline independently. Rate holds across the cohort.
Got Lucky
1 partner produced. Others didn't. That's a person, not a type.
06
When do you expand to a second partner type?
Ready
First type producing, subsystems codified, capacity exists.
Not Ready
First type still validating. Adding a second doubles complexity with zero signal.
